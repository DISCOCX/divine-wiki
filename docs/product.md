# Product

## Divine Skins

Platform for **League of Legends custom skins** — community-made visual mods. Browse, download, install. Everything is free. Monetized through ads and the Divine+ subscription.

- Website: **divineskins.gg**
- Launcher: **Celestial** (Windows app that installs/toggles skins in-game)
- Community: **Discord** (60K+ members — where support and discussion happen)
- Wiki: this project (replacing the legacy Divine Academy wiki)

The wiki sits alongside the web and launcher as reference / help content. It's public, anonymous-readable, no auth, no forum — community discussion lives in Discord.

## What custom skins are

Fan-made, **cosmetic-only** visual mods for LoL. Change how champions, abilities, particles, maps, and HUD elements look.

- **Client-side.** Only you see them. Teammates and enemies see the default.
- **No gameplay advantage.** Hitboxes, damage, timers unchanged.
- **Safe outside Korea and China.** No recorded bans since 2014 when installed through trusted tools. KR/CN anti-cheat blocks all mods — never recommend them there.
- **Patch-sensitive.** LoL patches every two weeks; skins occasionally break and creators push fixes. Normal.
- **Distributed as `.fantome` files.** Celestial handles install.

**Not the same as:** skin hacks / skin changers (tools that unlock Riot's paid skins — bannable) or cheats (gameplay-altering — bannable). Don't conflate.

## Audience

Male gamers, 16–24, PC-first, anime/gaming culture. Biggest markets: **France, Turkey, Brazil, US** — plus strong pockets in SEA and Eastern Europe. Most are non-native English speakers, so write plainly: short sentences, simple words, no idioms.
